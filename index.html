01How This Roadmap Is Organized

50 days is tight for "zero knowledge → confident competitor," so this plan is triage-based: it front-loads Web and Crypto (fastest points-per-hour for beginners), builds solid Linux/Networking fundamentals first since everything depends on them, treats Reverse Engineering and Forensics as "learn the easy 60%," and folds Misc/OSINT into the final stretch where pattern-recognition matters more than weeks of study.

06CTF Write-up Learning Guide

Why write-ups accelerate learning: they compress hours of someone else's trial-and-error into a 10-minute read, and repeated exposure to solution patterns builds intuition faster than solving everything from scratch.

Avoiding spoilers: attempt genuinely for 30–45 minutes first; read only the write-up for the specific challenge you're stuck on.

Notes per solved challenge: name/category/points, your first three ideas (even wrong ones), the actual technique, exact payloads used verbatim, and one line on what you'd try first next time.

Reproduce, don't just read: rebuild the environment when possible and actually run the exploit yourself.

Knowledge base: Obsidian or Notion (both free), one note per challenge, tagged by technique, with a master index.

Searching GitHub: <challenge name> writeup or <CTF name> <year> writeup github.

Common mistakes: reading the full write-up too early, not saving reusable payloads, skipping "why did this work," and only practicing your strong categories.

10FAQ

Falling behind schedule? Cut Reverse Engineering depth first, then Forensics. Never skip Web/Crypto review days.

Specialize or stay broad? Stay broad through Day 35, specialize afterward based on your Week 7 self-assessment — breadth wins more points in beginner CTFs than narrow depth.

Need a home lab? A free VirtualBox + Ubuntu/Kali VM covers everything in this plan.

Stuck for hours? Cap it at 45–60 minutes, then check a hint/write-up and move on.
